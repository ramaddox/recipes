# Chicken Gnocchi Soup

A creamy crockpot chicken and gnocchi soup finished with crispy bacon and fresh spinach. Cozy, filling, and mostly hands-off.

## Ingredients

- 1 lb chicken breasts
- 2 cups mirepoix (onion, celery, carrot)
- 1–2 tsp dried basil
- 1–2 tsp Italian seasoning
- 1 tsp poultry seasoning
- 1 tsp salt
- 4 cups chicken broth
- 3 tbsp cornstarch
- 2 (12 oz) cans evaporated milk
- 2 (1 lb) packages gnocchi
- 6 slices bacon
- 2–3 cloves garlic, minced
- 5 oz fresh baby spinach

## Steps

1. Place the chicken, mirepoix, basil, Italian seasoning, poultry seasoning, salt, and broth in a crockpot. Cover and cook on low for 6–8 hours, then shred the chicken directly in the crockpot.
2. Whisk the cornstarch into a little of the evaporated milk, then add it along with the rest of the evaporated milk and the gnocchi. Stir, cover, and cook for 45 minutes to 1 hour.
3. While the soup thickens, cut the bacon into small pieces and fry until crispy. Drain on paper towels and wipe the grease from the pan. Add the garlic and sauté for 1 minute, then add the spinach and stir until wilted. Stir the bacon and spinach into the crockpot.
4. Add more liquid as needed (such as 1 cup water) if the soup gets too thick. Season with salt and pepper to taste, then serve.
