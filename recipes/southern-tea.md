# The Art of Southern Tea

Three ingredients, six drinks, one simple ritual. This brews a pitcher of black tea (about 2.25 liters) that becomes sweet or unsweet — and the base for every drink on the menu.

## The Three-Ingredient Pantry

- **Sweet tea** — brewed and sugared
- **Unsweet tea** — plain brewed
- **Lemonade** — fresh squeezed

From these three you can pour six menu items: sweet tea, unsweet tea, lemonade, half & half (sweet/unsweet tea), Arnold Palmer (tea + lemonade), and unsweet tea finished with simple syrup to order.

## Ingredients

- 3 black tea bags
- 2.25 liters fresh, cold spring or filtered water (1 L to steep + 1.25 L to add)
- 201g sugar (for sweet tea; omit for unsweet)

## Steps

1. **Start with fresh water.** Measure 1 liter of cold, filtered or spring water. Avoid re-boiling, as water that has sat or been boiled before loses oxygen and makes tea taste flat.
2. **Heat to the right temperature.** Bring the water to a full, rolling boil, 194–212°F (90–100°C). Black tea holds up well to fully boiling water, unlike green or white.
3. **First steep.** Place the 3 tea bags in your pitcher. Pour the hot water directly over them, pressing gently so they're submerged. Cover and steep 20 minutes.
4. **Second steep.** Add 1.25 liters of cool spring water. Cover and steep another 15–20 minutes. This deepens the flavor while gently bringing the temperature down.
5. **Remove bags and sweeten while warm.** Lift out the tea bags without squeezing, which releases bitter tannins. For sweet tea, stir in the 201g sugar while the tea is still warm so it dissolves fully. For unsweet, skip this and chill as-is.
6. **Mix and serve.** Refrigerate until chilled, then pour any of the six menu items to order.

## Notes

- **Don't squeeze the bags.** Squeezing releases harsh tannins that turn tea bitter. Lift and discard, letting gravity do the work.
- **Sweeten while warm.** Sugar won't dissolve in cold tea. Add it while warm and stir until fully incorporated before chilling.
- **Simple syrup on the side.** Keep a small bottle of simple syrup on hand so guests can sweeten individual glasses of unsweet tea to their own taste.
