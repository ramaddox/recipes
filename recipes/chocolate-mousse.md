# Chocolate Mousse

A rich, classic French chocolate mousse topped with whipped cream and chocolate shavings. Light, airy, and deeply chocolatey.

## Ingredients

- 3 tbsp unsalted butter
- 6 oz semisweet chocolate, best quality
- 3 large eggs, room temperature, yolks and whites separated
- 1/2 tsp cream of tartar
- 1/4 cup plus 2 tbsp sugar
- 1/2 cup heavy cream, cold
- 1/2 tsp vanilla extract

**For serving**

- 1/2 cup heavy cream, cold
- 2 tsp sugar
- Chocolate shavings

## Steps

1. Place the butter in a medium bowl. Break the chocolate into small pieces and melt it with the butter over low heat. Let cool a few minutes, then whisk in the egg yolks one at a time, mixing until smooth after each. Set aside.
2. In a separate bowl, beat the egg whites on medium-high until foamy. Add the cream of tartar and beat until soft peaks form. Gradually beat in 1/4 cup of the sugar and continue until stiff peaks form. Using a large rubber spatula, fold the egg whites into the chocolate mixture until uniform. Set aside.
3. In another bowl, beat the 1/2 cup heavy cream on medium-high until it begins to thicken. Add the remaining 2 tbsp sugar and the vanilla and beat until it holds medium peaks. Fold the whipped cream into the chocolate mixture until fully incorporated, but don't overmix. Divide among 6 glasses, cover, and chill until set, at least 2 hours.
4. Up to a few hours before serving, whip the 1/2 cup cream until it thickens. Add the 2 tsp sugar and whip to medium peaks. Dollop over the mousse and top with chocolate shavings.
