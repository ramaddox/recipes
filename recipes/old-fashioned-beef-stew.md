# Old Fashioned Beef Stew

A classic red-wine beef stew with carrots and potatoes, simmered until fork-tender.

## Ingredients

- 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
- 1/4 tsp freshly ground pepper
- 1 lb beef stewing meat, trimmed and cut into 1-inch cubes
- 5 tsp vegetable oil
- 2 tbsp red wine vinegar
- 1 cup red wine
- 3 1/2 cups beef broth, low sodium
- 2 bay leaves
- 1 medium onion, peeled and chopped
- 5 medium carrots, peeled and cut into 1/4-inch rounds
- 2 large baking potatoes, peeled and cubed
- 2 tsp salt

## Steps

1. Combine the flour and pepper in a bowl. Add the beef and toss to coat well. Heat 3 tsp of the oil in a large pot. Add the beef a few pieces at a time (do not overcrowd) and cook, turning, until browned on all sides, about 5 minutes per batch.
2. Remove the beef and add the vinegar and wine. Cook over medium-high heat, scraping the pan to loosen the browned bits. Return the beef, then add the beef broth and bay leaves. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a slow simmer.
3. Cover and cook, skimming from time to time, until the beef is tender, about 1 1/2 hours. Add the onions and carrots and simmer, covered, 10 minutes. Add the potatoes and simmer until the vegetables are tender, about 30 minutes more. Add broth or water if the stew gets dry. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
4. Serve.
